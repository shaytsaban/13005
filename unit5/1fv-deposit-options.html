Question 1 - Investment Options Analysis, Using FV Calculations

An investor commits to depositing 5,000 ILS at the start of each year for 7 years. They must choose between two savings plans:
- Option A: A savings account with a fixed annual interest rate of 5%.
- Option B: A savings account with variable rates: 3% per year for the first 3 years, 8% per year for years 4 and 5, and 4.5% per year for years 6 and 7.
Assuming the investor makes a rational decision, what is the maximum amount they can expect to accumulate after 7 years?

üìù Solution & Explanation:

This problem requires calculating the Future Value (FV) of an annuity due, as payments are made at the beginning of each year. We‚Äôll evaluate both options to determine the maximum FV, breaking down the calculations with clear steps and equations. The first payment is made at time t=0 (start of year 1), and the FV is assessed at the end of year 7 (t=7).

Option A: Fixed 5% Interest Rate

For a constant 5% annual interest rate over 7 years with 5,000 ILS deposited annually at the start, we use the FV of an annuity due formula:

FV = PMT √ó [(1 + r)^t - 1] / r

Where:
- PMT = 5,000 ILS (annual payment)
- r = 0.05 (5% interest rate)
- t = 7 (number of years)
Note: This formula provides the FV as of the last deposit date (end of year 6 in this case). Since the investor withdraws the funds at the end of year 7, an adjustment is required to compound the value forward one additional year using (1 + r)^t, where t = 1.

FV at end of year 6 = 5,000 √ó [(1.05)^7 - 1] / 0.05
= 5,000 √ó [1.407100422 - 1] / 0.05
= 5,000 √ó 0.407100422 / 0.05
= 5,000 √ó 8.14200844
= 40,710.04 ILS

Adjust to end of year 7:

FV at end of year 7 = 40,710.04 √ó (1.05)^1
= 40,710.04 √ó 1.05
= 42,745.54 ILS

Thus, Option A results in approximately 42,745.54 ILS after 7 years.

Option B: Variable Interest Rates

Option B features variable rates over the 7-year period, applied as follows:
- Years 1-3 (t=0 to t=3): 3%
- Years 4-5 (t=3 to t=5): 8%
- Years 6-7 (t=5 to t=7): 4.5%
Each payment is made at the start of a year and compounds to the end of year 7 (t=7) based on the rates for the years it spans.

FV for each payment:

  • Payment at t=0 (start of year 1): 7 years (3 years at 3%, 2 years at 8%, 2 years at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.03)^3 √ó (1.08)^2 √ó (1.045)^2
    = 5,000 √ó 1.092727 √ó 1.1664 √ó 1.092025
    = 5,000 √ó 1.392497
    = 6,962.49 ILS
  • Payment at t=1 (start of year 2): 6 years (2 years at 3%, 2 years at 8%, 2 years at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.03)^2 √ó (1.08)^2 √ó (1.045)^2
    = 5,000 √ó 1.0609 √ó 1.1664 √ó 1.092025
    = 5,000 √ó 1.352425
    = 6,762.12 ILS
  • Payment at t=2 (start of year 3): 5 years (1 year at 3%, 2 years at 8%, 2 years at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.03) √ó (1.08)^2 √ó (1.045)^2
    = 5,000 √ó 1.03 √ó 1.1664 √ó 1.092025
    = 5,000 √ó 1.311094
    = 6,555.47 ILS
  • Payment at t=3 (start of year 4): 4 years (2 years at 8%, 2 years at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.08)^2 √ó (1.045)^2
    = 5,000 √ó 1.1664 √ó 1.092025
    = 5,000 √ó 1.273879
    = 6,369.40 ILS
  • Payment at t=4 (start of year 5): 3 years (1 year at 8%, 2 years at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.08) √ó (1.045)^2
    = 5,000 √ó 1.08 √ó 1.092025
    = 5,000 √ó 1.179267
    = 5,896.34 ILS
  • Payment at t=5 (start of year 6): 2 years (2 years at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.045)^2
    = 5,000 √ó 1.092025
    = 5,460.12 ILS
  • Payment at t=6 (start of year 7): 1 year (1 year at 4.5%)
    FV = 5,000 √ó (1.045)
    = 5,225.00 ILS

Total FV for Option B:

FV_B = 6,962.49 + 6,762.12 + 6,555.47 + 6,369.40 + 5,896.34 + 5,460.12 + 5,225.00
= 43,230.94 ILS

Thus, Option B yields approximately 43,230.94 ILS after 7 years.

üéØ Conclusion

Comparing the results:
- Option A: 42,745.54 ILS
- Option B: 43,230.94 ILS
The rational choice is Option B, offering the maximum amount of 43,230.94 ILS after 7 years.
